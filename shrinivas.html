Identified Business Ideas

Night-Time Safe Travel Services

Programs, transport options and safety tools that help people move safely after dark. These services reduce risk and provide support for vulnerable users.

  • Safe-Walk Escorts: Trained personnel accompany users for short last-mile walks.
  • Night Shuttles / Late-Night Buses: Dedicated routes operating after hours.
  • Ride-Share Safety Features: Trip sharing, PIN checks, emergency help integrated into apps.
  • Safe Havens / Safe Spots: Well-lit, monitored locations for waiting or refuge.
Drone-Based Fast Delivery Network

A network of small unmanned aerial vehicles for rapid, low-latency parcel delivery with reduced road congestion.

  • Faster delivery times by avoiding traffic.
  • Suitable for urgent medical supplies and last-mile logistics.
  • Requires airspace management and local regulation.
HealthTech: Smart Emergency Response Systems

Integrates IoT, AI and real-time coordination to improve emergency response times and outcomes.

  • AI-assisted dispatch and GPS tracking for faster response.
  • Wearables and home sensors enable automatic incident reporting.
  • Improves coordination between ambulances, hospitals and emergency services.
Smart Farming Zone with Hydroponics

Soilless agriculture using IoT sensors and automated controls to optimize growth and resource use.

  • Hydroponic systems supply nutrients directly in water.
  • IoT sensors monitor pH, moisture, temperature and light.
  • Automated control of nutrients and environment increases yield and reduces waste.

Course Reflection

1. Problems in Nature: Iteration, Recursion, and Backtracking

Nature offers many patterns that directly relate to algorithmic thinking, and these patterns can inspire technological solutions like the ones in the four projects.

  • Iteration: Natural cycles such as seasons repeat predictably. This iterative idea appears in the Drone-Based Fast Delivery Network, where drones follow delivery routes, return to hubs, recharge, and restart the cycle.
  • Recursion: Seen in plant branching or shell patterns. Smart Farming Zones with IoT monitor many small units (plant trays, sensor nodes) in a self-similar way, forming a recursive monitoring system.
  • Backtracking: Animals retrace steps when paths fail. Night-Time Safe Travel Services recalculate safer paths when a route is unsafe or unavailable. Emergency Response Systems also recalculate routes if ambulances encounter obstacles.

2. Space and Time Efficiency

Efficiency is essential in every computational project.

  • Time efficiency: How fast an algorithm executes.
  • Space efficiency: How much memory an algorithm requires.

Relevance to the projects:

  • Drone Networks: Require near-real-time route optimization (Dijkstra, A*) and space-efficient onboard algorithms.
  • Smart Farming with IoT: Continuous sensor data needs low-memory, fast analytics.
  • Emergency Response Systems: Time efficiency directly affects outcomes in life-critical flows.
  • Night-Time Safe Travel Services: Depend on efficient graph traversal and shortest-path methods for real-time tracking and rerouting.

Orders of Growth (examples):

  • O(1): Instant operations (sensor reads).
  • O(log n): Searching in sorted logs.
  • O(n): Scanning lists of drones or routes.
  • O(n log n) / O(n²): Sorting large datasets.

3. Design Principles and Their Role

Algorithmic design principles map directly to system components:

  • DFS / BFS: DFS for deep exploratory routing; BFS for safe, short-path discovery.
  • String algorithms (Boyer-Moore, KMP): Useful for parsing logs and alerts in sensors and emergency messages.
  • Balancing (AVL / Red-Black): Essential for databases backing drone hubs, crop data, and emergency call logs to ensure fast updates and queries.

4. Hierarchical Data & Tree Structures

Hierarchical structures are used across the projects:

  • Drone Delivery: Delivery zones, warehouses and route hierarchies.
  • Smart Farming: Plants, sensors and trays organized hierarchically.
  • Emergency Response: Hospitals, ambulances and routing trees.
  • Night-Time Safe Travel: Route hierarchies and map partitions.

5. Need for Array Query Algorithms

Range queries (Segment Trees, BITs) and lookup tables enable fast, real-time decisions across the systems — from weather slices for drones to nutrient ranges for farms.

6. Trees vs. Graphs and Traversals

Use trees for hierarchical indexing and graphs for routing problems. Traversal techniques (preorder/inorder/postorder, DFS, BFS) support systematic data inspection and routing decisions.

7. Sorting & Searching in Practice

Sorting and searching keep the systems responsive: prioritizing drone deliveries, filtering sensor alerts, and finding nearest hubs or safe zones.

8. Spanning Trees & Shortest Paths

Spanning trees minimize network cost (drone station links, utility planning). Shortest path algorithms (Dijkstra, A*, Floyd-Warshall) are fundamental for routing and safety planning.

9. Algorithm Design Techniques

  • Backtracking: Route recalculation when a path fails.
  • Divide and Conquer: Partitioning large delivery or farm zones.
  • Dynamic Programming: Caching repeated calculations for routing and prediction.
  • Graph Traversal Techniques: Core to navigation, scheduling and monitoring.
