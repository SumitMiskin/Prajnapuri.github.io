Drone-Based Fast Delivery Network

A localized drone delivery system for last-mile logistics — suitable for medical supplies, small parcels, and urgent deliveries with optimized landing zones and air corridors.

Logistics workflow (visual)

Problem

  • Traffic and long last-mile times slow urgent deliveries.
  • Remote or congested neighborhoods are under-served.

Solution

  • Small autonomous drones for rapid short-range deliveries.
  • Designated micro-hubs and safe landing pads integrated with local businesses.
  • Air corridor planning to avoid no-fly zones and ensure safety.

Implementation notes

  • Start with pre-approved corridors and line-of-sight operations.
  • Partner with health services for medical deliveries as an initial use-case.

How It Works

  1. Customer places an order
  2. Orders are prioritized based on urgency
  3. Shortest route is calculated
  4. Nearest drone is assigned
  5. Real-time tracking and delivery

Algorithms Used

  • Dijkstra’s Algorithm: Finds shortest delivery path
  • Kruskal’s Algorithm: Builds cost-efficient hub network
  • Quick Sort: Prioritizes orders
  • Heap / Priority Queue: Handles urgent deliveries
  • Segment Tree & Fenwick Tree: Track demand and drone activity
  • KMP Algorithm: Analyzes customer feedback
